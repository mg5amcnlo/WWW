Single-top t-channel production in the four- and five-flavour scheme

  • Description:
      Event files for t-channel single-top production in the four- and five-flavour scheme at the LHC and at the Tevatron. No top decays are included. The bottom is considered as massive in the four-flavour scheme, and massless in the five-flavour scheme. Input parameters are the following:

      • αEW = 1/132.338 GeV,
      • mW = 80.398 GeV,
      • mb = 4.75 GeV in the four-flavour scheme,
      • mb = 0 in the five-flavour scheme,
      • mt = 172.5 GeV,
      • sin2 θW = 0.22265,
      • MSTW2008(N)LO_nf4 for the (N)LO in the four-flavour scheme,
      • MSTW2008(N)LO for the (N)LO in the five-flavour scheme,
      • μR = μF = 4 sqrt(mb2+pT,b2) in the four-flavour scheme,
      • μR = μF = mtop in the five-flavour scheme.
  • Event files:
      The NLO events are generated with the MC subtraction terms for Herwig 6. The cross sections are given below, and the uncertainty is from the MC integration only. As they are, the NLO events are still non-physical: they need to be showered with Herwig 6 to be meaningful.

      • LHC 8 TeV: 4F NLO (51.65 +/- 0.08 pb), 4F LO (44.69 +/- 0.04 pb), 5F NLO (55.48 +/- 0.07 pb)

      • LHC 7 TeV: 4F NLO (39.37 +/- 0.05 pb), 4F LO (33.740 +/- 0.008 pb), 5F NLO (42.338 +/- 0.015 pb)

      • Tevatron: 4F NLO (0.9195 +/- 0.0017 pb), 4F LO (0.76573 +/- 0.00018 pb), 5F NLO (1.0077 +/- 0.0005 pb)

      The codes relevant to the generation of POWHEG samples for the same process can be found here

  • Technical details:
      The NLO event files use the UseSudakov=.true. and DampMCsubt=.true., using the default values of the code. In the five-flavour computation diagrams not featuring a bottom quark in the initial state are not imporved by finite mb effects. Analytic virtuals from arXiv:0903.0005
