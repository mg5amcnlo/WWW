W+ + 1 jet production

  • Description:
      Event files for the production of a W+-boson in association with a jet. The decays of the W-boson to positrons and neutrinos are included (with spin correlations and off-shell effects). Input parameters are the following:

      • mW = 80.419 GeV,
      • ΓW = 2.0476 GeV,
      • αEW = 1/132.507 GeV,
      • mZ = 91.188 GeV,
      • diagonal CKM matrix,
      • MSTW2008(N)LO for the (N)LO,
      • μR = μF = sqrt(mlν2+pT,lν2) (i.e. W-boson transverse mass)
      • jets are defined with anti-kT algorithm with R = 0.4 (and minimum pT defined below)
  • Event files:
      The NLO events are generated with the MC subtraction terms for Herwig 6 and the files contain 5 million events and are for LHC 7 TeV. As they are, the NLO events are still non-physical: they need to be showered with herwig 6 to be meaningful.

      • pT(j) > 2.5 GeV: NLO Herwig

      • pT(j) > 5 GeV: NLO Herwig

      • pT(j) > 10 GeV: NLO Herwig (has only 4999999 events)

      • pT(j) > 25 GeV: NLO Herwig

  • Plots:
      The plot below shows the transverse momentum of the hardest jet in W + jet events at the MC@NLO level. The colors correspond to different cuts at the generation level, i.e. from the 4 event files above. The black line is the fixed order NLO result. At the analysis level the jets have been reconstructed with a minimum pT(j) cut at 2.5 GeV.

      The lower inset of the plot clearly shows the onset of the coloured curves: at about 20 GeV the blue and the red curves coincide, at about 40 GeV the green curve as well and at 80-100 GeV also the magenta curves lies on top of the other three. This clearly shows that for a correct description of events at NLO accuracy the cut on the pT at the generation level should be about a factor 4 smaller than the cut at the analysis level. At low pT the discrepancy between the fixed order and the aMC@NLO results is non-negligible (although always within a quite reasonable 20%). This is no real surprise given that non-perturbative corrections become sizable here. For jets with a pT larger than 15 GeV the difference is already down to 10%.

      Also for other observables and distributions the differences between the four event files and the fixed order results can easily be explained. The factor 4 in cuts at the generation level versus cuts at the analysis level found in the plot above also applies to all these distributions, as expected. In fact, for many observables a smaller value is sufficient, greatly increasing the efficiency of the cuts: the difference between the generation level and analysis level is smaller and therefore a larger cut can be applied at the generation level to have an unbiased sample at the analysis level.

  • Technical details:
      The NLO event files use the UseSudakov=.true. and DampMCsubt=.true., using the default values of the code in revision 246. technical cut has been applied to the electron-neutriono invariant mass: events for which the invariant mass is more than 30 times the W-boson width away from its mass have been removed. All the events have the (off-shell) W-boson explicitly in the event record as intermediate particle, so its invariant mass should be conserved by Herwig. The event files were generated on May 31, 2011.
